# Marketplace Add-ons

> Add-ons are premium capabilities layered on top of modules a paid business already has.
> Modules give the daily workflow. Add-ons give automation, analytics, integrations, approvals, AI, and scale.

---

## Marketplace Rule

Add-ons should make the product feel advanced without making the core product complicated.

Use add-ons for features that:

- Save repeated owner effort through automation.
- Need third-party integrations or platform approvals.
- Add advanced reporting or forecasting.
- Require approval flows, audit depth, or sensitive controls.
- Help larger teams, more stock, more locations, or more compliance needs.
- Consume AI credits, storage, messaging volume, or other paid infrastructure.

Do not use add-ons to fix a broken core workflow. If the core module cannot complete its basic job without a feature, that feature belongs in `docs/v1-core-scope.md`.

Free businesses cannot activate add-ons. Starter, Growth, and Pro businesses can activate add-ons only for modules unlocked by their current plan.

## Entitlement Interaction

Plans unlock modules. Add-ons unlock premium capabilities inside modules.

Free cannot buy add-ons. An add-on should not unlock a locked module by itself. For example, HR Payroll can be activated only when the business has HR access, and Marketing automation should not appear until Marketing is unlocked.

---

## Implementation Notes

The marketplace catalog in code may start smaller than this product catalog. When adding a new add-on:

1. Use a stable kebab-case slug.
2. Attach it to one module where possible.
3. Decide whether billing is monthly, yearly, one-time, or included.
4. Add the seed/migration row only when the feature is ready to sell or configure.
5. Keep planned add-ons visible in product docs, but do not imply they are live until the implementation is shipped.
