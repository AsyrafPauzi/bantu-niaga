# Module 3 — Operations

> Move work from order to delivery while keeping products, suppliers, stock, and bookings organized.

## 1. Purpose

Operations helps owners understand what work is pending, what is in progress, what is ready, and what has been delivered. It should be flexible enough for product, service, booking, and small fulfillment businesses without becoming a warehouse system in core.

## 2. Target Users

- Solo entrepreneurs who sell products or services and need order tracking.
- Micro SMEs with suppliers, simple stock, and booking needs.
- Growing SMEs that later need inventory automation, multi-location stock, purchase orders, and scheduling.

## 3. Core Features

Core Operations should cover basic workflow organization:

- Product and service catalogue.
- SKU, category, price, and image fields.
- Supplier directory.
- Supplier contact and payment terms.
- Basic order pipeline.
- Order notes.
- Pickup or delivery status.
- Booking calendar.
- Service appointment slots.
- Basic stock quantity.
- Low-stock warning.

## 4. Add-on Features

Operations add-ons should carry scale, automation, and richer scheduling:

- Product Variants.
- Customer Booking Page.
- Booking Buffer Automation.
- Advanced Inventory.
- Auto Stock Deduction.
- Multi-Location Stock.
- Purchase Order Generator.
- Auto Reorder Reminder.
- Supplier Cost Analytics.
- Resource Scheduling.
- Operations AI Planner.

## 5. Key User Flows

### 5.1 Track an order

1. Owner creates an order card.
2. Owner adds customer, products or services, notes, and due date.
3. Owner moves the card from New to In Progress, Ready, and Delivered.
4. Owner can link the order to an invoice when payment is needed.

### 5.2 Manage a supplier

1. Owner adds supplier name, contact, payment terms, and notes.
2. Owner records what the supplier usually provides.
3. Owner reviews supplier details before restocking or placing an order.

### 5.3 Record a booking

1. Owner opens the booking calendar.
2. Owner chooses service, date, time, and customer.
3. Booking appears on the calendar.
4. Owner updates status after completion or cancellation.

## 6. Data Notes

Core tables should cover products, services, suppliers, orders, order stages, bookings, and basic stock quantity. The core stock model should stay simple and not promise full inventory accounting.

Premium add-ons can extend the model with variants, stock movements, reorder rules, purchase orders, supplier cost history, public booking links, buffer rules, resource assignments, and AI-generated planning notes.
