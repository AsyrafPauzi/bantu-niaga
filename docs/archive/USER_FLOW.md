## 1. How to Read This Document

This document explains Bantu Niaga through user flows, not only feature lists. A user flow shows what a real business owner, manager, cashier, staff member, or marketer does from morning to night inside the system.

Bantu Niaga is a business operating system for Malaysian micro-SMEs. It helps small businesses move away from scattered WhatsApp chats, paper receipts, Excel sheets, phone galleries, and disconnected apps into one structured account that grows with the business.

### Desktop and Mobile

Bantu Niaga is designed around two working modes:

- **Desktop ERP control layer:** used for setup, reporting, configuration, analytics, team control, billing, compliance review, and business planning.
- **Mobile PWA execution layer:** used for daily action, such as sending invoices, recording expenses, checking orders, updating tasks, approving leave, sharing links, and using WhatsApp-first workflows.

In simple terms:

> The owner controls the business from desktop, but runs the business from the phone.

### Modules Unlock by Plan

Bantu Niaga has six core modules:

- **Finance:** invoices, expenses, ledger, payment tracking, simple money visibility.
- **Admin:** documents, storage, tasks, reminders, business administration.
- **Operations:** orders, products, suppliers, bookings, service workflows.
- **Sales:** counter sales, POS flow, leads, follow-ups.
- **HR:** staff records, leave, roles, people management.
- **Marketing:** customer CRM, segments, content planning, media, broadcasts, coupons.

The plan determines which modules a business can use. Cross-cutting areas such as Home, Settings, Marketplace, Boardroom surfaces, and mobile More are treated as shared areas, although certain advanced actions may still depend on plan, add-on, or roadmap readiness.

> Pricing in this document follows the current app plan catalog. It can still be adjusted commercially before final public launch.

## 3. Shared First-Time Onboarding Flow

Every plan starts with the same idea: create one clean business account, then unlock the right tools based on the plan.

### First-Time Journey

1. **Owner signs up**
   - The owner creates an account using email or supported login method.
   - The system treats this person as the business owner.
   - The owner is the highest-permission user for billing, team, settings, and unlocked modules.

2. **Owner creates the business profile**
   - Add business name, business type, contact details, address, and preferred business identity.
   - Example: "Kak Mira Home Bakery", "Kedai Runcit Seri Maju", "Ayu Beauty Salon", or "Zam Gadget Store".
   - This profile becomes the base identity used across invoices, documents, customer records, and shared links.

3. **Owner chooses a plan**
   - Free starts with Finance.
   - Plus unlocks Finance, Admin, and Operations.
   - Growth adds Sales and HR.
   - Pro unlocks all six modules including Marketing.

4. **Owner lands on the Home dashboard**
   - Home shows the main areas available to that business.
   - Locked modules can be shown as upgrade opportunities so clients understand what comes next.
   - The dashboard is meant to answer, "What needs attention today?"

5. **Owner sets up the first meaningful record**
   - Free: first customer, first invoice, or first expense.
   - Plus: first receipt upload, first task, first supplier, first product, or first order stage.
   - Growth: first staff member, first cashier role, first lead, or first POS sale.
   - Pro: first customer import, first segment, first content plan, first broadcast, or first coupon.

6. **Owner learns the desktop and mobile split**
   - Use desktop to configure and review.
   - Use mobile to act quickly during daily operations.
   - Example: create products on desktop, send invoice from mobile, review weekly cash position on desktop.

7. **Owner gets support through WhatsApp-friendly behavior**
   - The product language should support how Malaysian SMEs already work.
   - Shared invoices, reminders, broadcasts, and follow-up messages should feel natural for WhatsApp-first communication.

8. **Roles and security are introduced**
   - The owner can add team members on eligible plans.
   - Each person gets only the access they need.
   - This matters because finance, staff records, and customer data should not be visible to every helper or cashier.

> Stakeholder takeaway: onboarding is not about filling forms. It is about getting the business from "manual and scattered" to "structured and ready to operate" as quickly as possible.

## 4. Free Plan User Flow - Solo Owner / Basic Finance

### Who It Is For

Free is for a solo owner who wants to start organizing money without paying upfront.

Typical users:

- Home baker taking orders through WhatsApp.
- Freelancer issuing simple invoices.
- Small online seller tracking basic cash in and cash out.
- Service provider who wants a clearer record than a notebook.

### Modules and Surfaces

Unlocked module:

- Finance

Common surfaces:

- Home
- Settings
- Marketplace or upgrade areas where applicable
- Mobile More page
- Boardroom-style surfaces where available, with plan or credit limitations as applicable

### Day 1 Setup Flow

1. Owner creates the business profile.
2. Owner confirms the Free plan.
3. Owner adds first customer.
4. Owner creates the first invoice or records the first expense.
5. Owner reviews the Finance dashboard to see money activity.
6. Owner tests sharing an invoice or payment reminder through WhatsApp.

### Daily Flow

1. **Customer places order through WhatsApp**
   - Example: "Kak, saya nak order kek birthday untuk Sabtu."
   - Owner opens Bantu Niaga on mobile.

2. **Owner creates invoice**
   - Select or add customer.
   - Add item name, quantity, amount, and due date.
   - Save invoice.

3. **Owner shares invoice**
   - Share secure invoice link through WhatsApp.
   - Customer sees amount and payment reference clearly.

4. **Customer pays**
   - Customer sends transfer proof or pays using the available payment instruction.
   - Owner marks invoice as paid.

5. **Finance record updates**
   - The invoice becomes part of the business money record.
   - Owner can later review paid and unpaid invoices.

6. **Owner records expense**
   - Example: flour, packaging, petrol, delivery cost, market supplies.
   - Owner records amount, category, date, and optional receipt photo.

### Weekly Flow

At the end of the week, the owner opens desktop or tablet and reviews:

- Total income recorded.
- Total expenses recorded.
- Unpaid invoices.
- Basic cash in and cash out.
- Customer list growth.
- Simple export or report, where available.

This helps the owner answer:

- "Did I actually make money this week?"
- "Who has not paid yet?"
- "What did I spend too much on?"
- "Which customers came back?"

### Mobile Example

A home baker receives a WhatsApp order while buying ingredients. She opens Bantu Niaga on her phone, creates an invoice, sends it to the customer, and records the ingredient receipt. She does not need to wait until night to update an Excel file.

### Desktop Example

At night, the same owner opens Bantu Niaga on desktop. She checks unpaid invoices, reviews expenses, and prepares a clearer picture of the week before buying more stock.

### Upgrade Triggers

Free users should consider upgrading when:

- They need to store more documents, receipts, or admin files.
- They need task tracking, order stages, supplier records, or product/service organization.
- They need staff accounts or want helpers to update work without seeing everything.
- They need POS, sales pipeline, HR records, or staff leave tracking.
- They want CRM, customer segments, content planning, broadcasts, or coupons.

Recommended next step:

- Upgrade to **Plus** for Admin and Operations.
- Upgrade to **Growth** if staff, Sales, or HR become important.
- Upgrade to **Pro** if Marketing and retention become the growth focus.

### Client Value Summary

Free gives a solo owner a clean starting point. It turns basic money activity into a simple business record without forcing the owner into complicated accounting software.

## 5. Plus Plan User Flow - Small Team Operations

### Who It Is For

Plus is for small teams that are no longer just tracking money. They now need basic back-office control and a way to manage work.

Typical users:

- Kedai or cafe with one or two helpers.
- Home business with many orders per week.
- Service provider managing bookings and documents.
- Small operator that needs supplier, product, receipt, and task structure.

### Modules

Unlocked modules:

- Finance
- Admin
- Operations

### Day 1 Setup Flow

1. Owner upgrades or starts on Plus.
2. Owner confirms business profile and operating details.
3. Owner uploads important documents.
   - Receipts
   - Supplier invoices
   - Licenses
   - Signed forms
   - Business PDFs
4. Owner creates the first task board.
   - To Do
   - Doing
   - Done
5. Owner adds suppliers, products, or services.
6. Owner sets order or booking stages.
   - New order
   - In progress
   - Ready
   - Delivered
7. Owner adds team members within the plan's seat limit.
8. Owner teaches helpers to update work from mobile.

### Daily Admin Flow

1. Helper uploads receipt or document.
2. Owner assigns a task.
   - Example: "Prepare 20 lunch packs before 11am."
3. Staff updates task status from mobile.
4. Owner checks notifications or dashboard.
5. Important files stay in the business account instead of being buried in WhatsApp.

> Current implementation note: Admin Digital Storage is shipped with a 100MB cap in the current implementation. Broader Admin workflows such as deeper templates, reminders, and task matrix expansion should be presented as intended workflow or roadmap where not yet fully available.

### Daily Operations Flow

1. New order enters the system.
2. Owner or staff moves the order to "In progress".
3. Staff prepares the item or service.
4. Order moves to "Ready".
5. Order is delivered, picked up, or completed.
6. Finance follows up with invoice or payment record.
7. Customer history becomes easier to trace.

Example for a cafe:

- Customer orders catering for 30 pax.
- Manager creates order and assigns kitchen task.
- Staff updates preparation status.
- Owner sends invoice.
- Payment is marked.
- Order is completed.

### Supplier and Product Flow

1. Owner adds supplier details.
2. Owner records basic product or service information.
3. Owner connects supplier purchases to expenses where practical.
4. Weekly review helps identify which suppliers, costs, or products need attention.

### Booking Flow

For service businesses such as salon, homestay, tuition, or repair service:

1. Owner records available slot or intended booking stage.
2. Customer asks through WhatsApp.
3. Owner confirms booking.
4. Staff sees the work schedule.
5. Completion can lead to invoice, payment, and future follow-up.

> Some advanced booking and stock flows may be roadmap depending on current implementation state. The plan describes the business workflow Bantu Niaga is designed to support.

### Weekly and Monthly Flows

Weekly:

- Review pending orders.
- Review overdue tasks.
- Check uploaded receipts and documents.
- Review supplier expenses.
- Review unpaid invoices.

Monthly:

- Prepare documents for accountant.
- Check business reminders.
- Review supplier reliability.
- Decide whether operations need more staff or better sales tools.

### Mobile Example

A helper receives new stock at the shop. She uploads the supplier receipt using her phone and updates the task status. The owner can see it without asking for screenshots in WhatsApp.

### Desktop Example

The owner reviews the week's orders on desktop, checks which tasks were delayed, downloads receipts for filing, and sees which invoices are still unpaid.

### Upgrade Triggers

Plus users should consider upgrading when:

- They need POS or cashier workflow.
- They need lead tracking and sales follow-up.
- They need staff records, leave approvals, or HR documents.
- They have more than 3 staff seats.
- They want customer retention, marketing content, broadcasts, coupons, or customer segments.

Recommended next step:

- Upgrade to **Growth** for Sales and HR.
- Upgrade to **Pro** when marketing and customer retention become central.

### Client Value Summary

Plus helps a small team stop relying on memory, scattered files, and constant WhatsApp checking. It gives the business a shared operating board for finance, admin, and daily work.

## 6. Growth Plan User Flow - Sales + HR Business

### Who It Is For

Growth is for SMEs that have moved beyond simple operations. They have staff, sales activity, customer follow-up, and people management needs.

Typical users:

- Salon with staff and appointments.
- Retail shop with cashier.
- Cafe or mini mart with counter sales.
- Service business with leads and follow-ups.
- Small company preparing for more structured HR.

### Modules

Unlocked modules:

- Finance
- Admin
- Operations
- Sales
- HR

### Day 1 Setup Flow

1. Owner confirms the Growth plan.
2. Owner adds staff and assigns roles.
3. Owner adds products or services.
4. Owner sets Sales workflow.
   - POS or counter sale flow.
   - Lead statuses such as New, Follow Up, Won, and Lost.
5. Owner creates HR employee records.
6. Owner sets basic leave or staff tracking flow.
7. Owner confirms who can see Finance, Sales, HR, Admin, and Operations.

### Daily Sales POS Flow

1. Customer buys at the counter.
2. Cashier opens Sales surface.
3. Cashier selects item or service.
4. Payment is recorded.
5. Receipt or confirmation is given.
6. Finance receives the sales record or payment context.
7. Owner can review sales later without giving cashier access to full finance or HR.

### Daily Lead CRM Flow

1. A lead comes from WhatsApp, Instagram, walk-in, referral, or phone call.
2. Staff records the lead.
3. Lead is marked as New.
4. Manager assigns follow-up.
5. Staff updates the lead after contact.
6. Lead becomes Won or Lost.
7. Won lead can become a customer or sale.

Example:

- A customer asks a salon about bridal makeup.
- Staff records the lead and expected package value.
- Manager follows up with quotation.
- Customer confirms booking.
- Sale and customer history are preserved.

### Daily HR Flow

1. Staff submits or requests leave.
2. HR Officer or Manager reviews the request.
3. Owner or Manager approves or rejects.
4. Leave calendar updates.
5. Staff record stays organized for future reference.

For non-technical users, this means:

- Staff no longer need to ask only through chat.
- Owner can see who is available.
- Sensitive staff records are kept away from general staff access.

### Finance and Operations Sync

The value of Growth is not only that it has more modules. The value is that sales, staff, operations, and money can be viewed together.

Example flow:

1. Sales record is created.
2. Payment status is updated.
3. Order or stock status can be reviewed.
4. Finance sees income context.
5. Owner reviews staff, sales, and operations from one business account.

> Current implementation note: Sales and HR are plan-gated and part of the product direction. Some full POS, lead, leave, and HR operations may be roadmap or in-progress depending on release state. Present the workflow as the intended Growth operating model unless confirmed shipped in the live environment.

### Role Flows

**Owner**

- Sees all unlocked modules.
- Controls billing, plan, team, and settings.
- Can review Finance, Admin, Operations, Sales, and HR.

**Manager**

- Controls daily operations and team work.
- Reviews Sales and Operations.
- Does not need billing ownership.

**Cashier**

- Uses POS or Sales surface.
- Records customer purchases.
- Does not see HR records, billing, or full finance.

**HR Officer**

- Manages employee records and leave.
- Can access HR-related documents.
- Does not need full Sales or billing access.

**Staff**

- Sees assigned work and self-service flows.
- Updates tasks or leave requests.
- Does not see sensitive business data.

**Accountant**

- Reviews finance-related records.
- Does not need HR, Sales POS, or team billing controls.

### Weekly Flow

Every week, the owner or manager reviews:

- Sales totals.
- Leads still waiting for follow-up.
- Staff leave calendar.
- Pending tasks and orders.
- Stock or product issues.
- Unpaid invoices.
- Documents needed for accounting.

### Monthly Flow

Every month, the business can prepare:

- Basic financial review.
- Staff attendance or leave review.
- Payroll preparation context, where applicable.
- Sales performance review.
- Operations bottleneck review.

### Upgrade Triggers

Growth users should consider upgrading when:

- They need customer CRM and deeper customer profiles.
- They want VIP, repeat, dormant, and at-risk segments.
- They want a content calendar for TikTok, Instagram, and Facebook.
- They want media uploads, broadcasts, coupons, and retention campaigns.
- They have unlimited team or customer needs.
- Marketing has become a serious growth function, not just occasional posting.

Recommended next step:

- Upgrade to **Pro** for Marketing and the full six-module system.

### Client Value Summary

Growth gives an SME more control over the front counter, follow-ups, staff, and money. It is suitable when the business is no longer just owner-operated and needs proper roles.

## 9. Role-Based User Flow

Role-based access is one of the most important stakeholder points because small businesses often share one phone, one password, or one WhatsApp group. That creates risk.

Bantu Niaga separates access by role so each person sees what they need and nothing unnecessary.

### Owner

The owner is the main decision-maker.

Can typically:

- See all unlocked modules.
- Manage plan and billing.
- Add or remove team members.
- Change settings.
- Review Finance, HR, Sales, Admin, Operations, and Marketing depending on plan.

Why it matters:

- Owner keeps full control without sharing full access with every staff member.

### Manager

The manager runs daily work.

Can typically:

- Review operations.
- Assign tasks.
- Check sales activity.
- Help coordinate staff.
- See business workflows needed for management.

Why it matters:

- Manager can run the business day to day without controlling billing or ownership settings.

### Accountant

The accountant focuses on money.

Can typically:

- Access Finance.
- Review invoices, payments, expenses, and ledger-style information.
- Prepare for reporting or accounting discussion.

Why it matters:

- Accountant does not need access to HR records, marketing contacts, or owner settings.

### HR Officer

The HR Officer handles people records.

Can typically:

- Access HR.
- Manage HR-related documents.
- Review leave or employee information.

Why it matters:

- Staff IC, bank details, contracts, and leave records are sensitive.

### Cashier

The cashier handles counter sales.

Can typically:

- Use POS or Sales surface.
- Record payment.
- Issue receipt or sale confirmation.

Why it matters:

- Cashier can sell without seeing full business finance, HR, or billing information.

### Staff

Staff members focus on their own work.

Can typically:

- See assigned tasks.
- Update work status.
- Use self-service leave flow where available.

Why it matters:

- Staff can participate in the system without exposing sensitive owner or company data.

> Stakeholder takeaway: role-based access helps the business become professional without becoming complicated.
